About US

Yes, a planner is vital to the planning process, helps reduce your stress, and eases your mind leading up to the big day.... but the most important attribute a wedding planner provides is the ability to make sure you are present in each and every moment on your wedding day. ...

At first thought, hiring a wedding planner might seem like a luxurious splurge. Of course, having someone to make your big day run as smoothly as possible might seem like a good idea, but at what cost? When it comes to making the most of your budget, a wedding planner can actually help you save more on your bottom line.

Most people who have been engaged know how difficult it can be to plan a wedding. Searching for the perfect wedding venue, finding vendors you can trust, staying within your wedding budget, the list goes on and on. Many people hear the name “wedding planner” and automatically think hiring one is something they can’t fit into their wedding budget. However, hiring a wedding planner can more often than not save you money when planning your wedding. Wedding coordinators are experts in taking your wedding vision and executing into an event you will remember for the rest of your life.

A professional wedding coordinator is one of the most fundamental parts of having an amazing wedding day, but beyond day-of coordination, you may not even realize the full scope of wedding planner duties. Along with their expert wedding planning advice, guidance, and organization, a wedding planner can handle all—and we mean all— the little things you may need an extra hand to cover.

When budgeting for a wedding, how much food will cost is on the forefront of many people's minds. After all, your guests have to eat. How much is food catering for a wedding? It depends on the cost per plate, but most receptions for 100 people cost around $5,000 to $10,000, with average cost being around $7,000.
